Who created the Gentrification Tax? The Tax was created by Gentrification Tax Action (GTA), a collective of artists and designers formed to campaign for a Gentrification Tax. Current members include: Sameer Farooq, an artist and documentary filmmaker. Kika Thorne, an artist. Adrian Blackwell, an artist and urbanist, teaching architecture at the University of Waterloo. Jane Mah Hutton, a landscape architect, teaching at Waterloo Architecture.

here is a differentiated subtitle

What will funds be used for? Income from the tax would be donated directly to local land trusts and housing cooperatives, ensuring that the profits of gentrification remain in the neighborhood to create and maintain deeply affordable locally managed housing.

Who do the funds go to, and who benefits from them? The funds are distributed to the Land Trust you designate within the Neighbourhood Land Trust (NLT) network in Toronto/Tkaronto. Our pilot project is with the Parkdale Neighbourhood Land Trust (PNLT), directly benefiting residents from the Parkdale neighbourhood in Toronto.

Is this a donation? No. This is a one-time voluntary tax based on the sale of your house. Please use our calculator to determine what your payment should be.

When do I pay the Tax? The tax is designed to be paid upon the sale of a property. As a percentage of the increase in its value. If you do not have plans to sell your house, you have the option to pay the tax annually or monthly in advance.
